# Bassist Sounds - Free VST Recreation Guide

## Overview

Bass sounds are crucial for the foundation of any mix. This guide covers famous bassists and how to recreate their tones using free plugins.

---

## Flea (Red Hot Chili Peppers)

### Signature Sound
- Aggressive slap bass
- Bright, punchy fingerstyle
- Active bass through tube amp
- Funk meets rock energy

### Original Gear
- Modulus Flea Bass, Music Man Stingray
- Gallien-Krueger, Mesa Boogie
- Very few effects - tone from fingers

### Free VST Chain
```
1. Ignite Amps Emissary (clean channel) OR direct
2. Analog Obsession BritPre (add slight grit)
3. ReaEQ for shaping
```

### REAPER FX Chain
```
ReaEQ:
- HPF at 40 Hz
- Boost 80-100 Hz (+3 dB) - fundamental
- Cut 200-300 Hz (-3 dB) - remove mud
- Boost 800 Hz - 1 kHz (+4 dB) - slap pop
- Boost 2-4 kHz (+3 dB) - string attack

ReaComp:
- Ratio: 4:1
- Attack: 10-20 ms (preserve slap transients)
- Release: 100-150 ms
```

### Tips
- Practice thumb slap + finger pop technique
- Mute unused strings
- Key songs: "Higher Ground", "Aeroplane", "Give It Away"

## Geddy Lee (Rush)

### Signature Sound
- Bright, trebly "clanky" tone
- Rickenbacker through overdriven amp
- Complex melodic bass lines
- Chorus and flanger effects

### Original Gear
- Rickenbacker 4001, Fender Jazz
- Ampeg SVT, Tech 21 SansAmp
- Chorus, flanger for specific songs

### Free VST Chain
```
1. Analog Obsession SVT (Ampeg emulation)
   OR: Ignite Emissary (clean with grit)
2. TAL-Chorus-LX (for chorus sections)
```

### REAPER FX Chain
```
ReaEQ:
- HPF at 50 Hz
- Cut 200-300 Hz (-2 dB)
- Boost 800 Hz - 1 kHz (+3 dB) - midrange growl
- Boost 2-3 kHz (+4 dB) - clank
- Boost 5-6 kHz (+2 dB) - string brightness

ReaComp:
- Ratio: 3:1
- Attack: 30 ms (let attack through)
```

### Tips
- Fingers close to bridge for brightness
- Learn to play melodic counterpoint
- Key songs: "Tom Sawyer", "YYZ", "The Spirit of Radio"

## Justin Chancellor (Tool)

### Signature Sound
- Dark, grinding low-end
- Distorted bass with chorus
- Wal bass through various effects
- Polyrhythmic playing

### Original Gear
- Wal MK2 bass
- Demeter preamp, Mesa Boogie
- Boss BF-2 Flanger, chorus

### Free VST Chain
```
1. Ignite Emissary (with some gain)
2. TAL-Chorus-LX (crucial for his sound)
3. NadIR with bass cabinet IR
```

### REAPER FX Chain
```
ReaEQ:
- HPF at 40 Hz
- Boost 60-80 Hz (+3 dB) - sub weight
- Cut 250-400 Hz (-3 dB)
- Boost 1-2 kHz (+2 dB) - growl
- Cut highs above 6 kHz (dark)

Blend: Clean DI + Distorted amp (parallel)
```

### Tips
- Use pick for attack
- Odd time signatures
- Key songs: "Schism", "Forty Six & 2", "Lateralus"

## James Jamerson (Motown)

### Signature Sound
- Deep, warm thump
- Flatwound strings
- P-Bass direct or through Ampeg B-15
- Legendary "feel" and groove

### Original Gear
- Fender Precision Bass (1962)
- Ampeg B-15 "Portaflex"
- Flatwound strings, high action

### Free VST Chain
```
1. Direct recording (DI)
2. Analog Obsession CHANNEV (Neve warmth)
3. Softube Saturation Knob (subtle warmth)
```

### REAPER FX Chain
```
ReaEQ:
- HPF at 40 Hz (gentle)
- Boost 80-100 Hz (+2-3 dB) - thump
- Cut 200-400 Hz (-2 dB) - clean up
- Gentle roll-off above 4 kHz (vintage)

ReaComp:
- Ratio: 4:1
- Attack: 20 ms
- Slow release (200-300 ms)
- Gentle, 3-4 dB GR
```

### Tips
- Flatwound strings essential for this sound
- Play with fingers, near neck pickup
- Key songs: "What's Going On", "Ain't No Mountain High Enough"

## John Entwistle (The Who)

### Signature Sound
- Lead bass playing
- Bright, aggressive tone
- Bi-amped (clean low + dirty high)
- Alembic/custom basses

### Original Gear
- Alembic, custom basses
- Marshall, Sunn amps
- Bi-amp setup

### Free VST Chain
```
BI-AMP SETUP:
Low channel:
1. Clean DI
2. LPF at 500 Hz

High channel:
1. Ignite Emissary (with gain)
2. HPF at 500 Hz

Blend together
```

### REAPER FX Chain
```
Create two tracks:
Track 1 (lows): LPF 500 Hz, clean
Track 2 (highs): HPF 500 Hz, distorted
Sum together for bi-amp sound
```

### Tips
- Lead bass mentality
- Play with aggression
- Key songs: "My Generation", "The Real Me"
